Experience the beautiful tradition of St. Joseph altars, where faith, family, and Sicilian heritage come together in abundance and gratitude.

What is a St. Joseph Altar?

St. Joseph altars are a cherished Sicilian tradition that arrived in New Orleans with Italian immigrants. Created as thanksgiving for prayers answered, these beautiful displays honor St. Joseph on March 19th.

Faith & Devotion

Honoring St. Joseph, patron saint of workers, fathers, and the Sicilian people, through prayer and thanksgiving.

Community & Family

Bringing together families and neighbors to share food, stories, and blessings in the spirit of generosity.

Abundance & Gratitude

Celebrating with elaborate displays of traditional Sicilian foods, blessed bread, and offerings for those in need.

"The St. Joseph altar is more than a display—it's a living prayer, a feast for the eyes and soul, and a testament to the enduring spirit of Sicilian heritage in New Orleans. Every item placed on the altar carries meaning, from the three-tiered structure representing the Holy Trinity to the blessed fava beans symbolizing good fortune."

New Orleans Heritage

The Sacred Tradition

In New Orleans, St. Joseph altars blend Sicilian customs with local culture, creating a unique celebration of faith and community.

Three-Tiered Altar

Representing the Holy Trinity, altars are built in three ascending levels adorned with white linens, candles, and religious statues.

Traditional Foods

Elaborate displays feature cookies, breads, pasta, seafood, and symbolic items like fava beans—all meatless in keeping with Lenten traditions.

St. Joseph's Day

Celebrated on March 19th, the feast honors St. Joseph with public altars open to all, embodying the spirit of hospitality and charity.

Blessed Bread

Specially baked blessed bread shaped into crosses, staffs, and other symbols are distributed to visitors as tokens of blessing.

The Legend

During a severe drought in Sicily, people prayed to St. Joseph for relief. When the rains came, they created elaborate altars of thanksgiving laden with food to share with the less fortunate—a tradition of gratitude that continues today.

Open to All

True to the spirit of St. Joseph's generosity, altars are open to everyone. Visitors are welcomed to view the altars, receive blessed bread, and share in a meal—no matter their background or beliefs.

Experience the Beauty

Each altar is a work of art, crafted with love and devotion by families keeping this sacred tradition alive.
