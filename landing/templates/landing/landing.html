Celebrating a Sacred Sicilian Tradition

Experience the beautiful tradition of St. Joseph altars, where faith, family, and Sicilian heritage come together in abundance and gratitude.

The St. Joseph Altar tradition dates back to the Middle Ages when Sicily experienced a severe drought. In desperation, the people prayed to St. Joseph to intercede for rain. When their prayers were answered, they built elaborate altars filled with food as offerings of gratitude.

Today, this beautiful tradition continues in New Orleans, where families and communities create stunning altars each March 19th, inviting all to partake in the abundance and blessings.
