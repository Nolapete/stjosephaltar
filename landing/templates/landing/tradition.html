The Legend

During a severe drought in Sicily, people prayed to St. Joseph for relief. When the rains came, they created elaborate altars of thanksgiving laden with food to share with the less fortunate—a tradition of gratitude that continues today.

Open to All

True to the spirit of St. Joseph's generosity, altars are open to everyone. Visitors are welcomed to view the altars, receive blessed bread, and share in a meal—no matter their background or beliefs.
